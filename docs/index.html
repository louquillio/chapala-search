How search works

Match % — how closely a post's meaning matches your search query. 100% would be identical. Above 70% is a strong match. This is computed in your browser using a semantic AI model; nothing is sent to a server.

Points — the total reactions (Likes + Thanks) that post received from forum members. Higher points means the community found it useful. 3+ points qualifies a post for indexing.

Ranking — posts are scored by a blend of match relevance (55%), community points (25%), and how recent the post is (20%). If your search terms appear literally in a post's text, that post gets a bonus boost. Results are grouped by thread, with the best-matching post shown first and additional posts from the same thread collapsed.

Coverage — the current index covers roughly 100 pages of the forum (~Jan 2024 to present). It runs entirely on your device — no data is sent anywhere for search.
